At the Hyatt Regency, the deadly change looked like a shortcut.

The walkway design used one continuous hanger rod from roof to fourth floor to second floor. In shop drawings, it became two shorter rods. Easier to build.

But the load path changed.

The fourth-floor connection now carried both walkways, not one. The load essentially doubled. NBS later found the original design was already weak; the revision left roughly one-third of code-expected capacity.

On July 17, 1981, two walkways collapsed during a crowded dance. Official records count 113 dead and 186 injured.

This was not a shop detail. It was a structural redesign.

Every handoff treated it like paperwork.

Small causes, massive consequences.
