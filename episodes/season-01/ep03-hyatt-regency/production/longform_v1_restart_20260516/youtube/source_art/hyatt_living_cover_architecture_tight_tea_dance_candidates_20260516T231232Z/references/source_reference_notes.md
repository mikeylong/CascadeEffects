Architecture facts used for candidate review:

- Three suspended walkways spanned the atrium at the second, third, and fourth floors.
- The second-floor walkway was suspended from the fourth-floor walkway, directly above it.
- The fourth-floor walkway was suspended from atrium roof framing by six hanger rods.
- The third-floor walkway was offset from the other two and was independently suspended from roof framing.
- The second and fourth floor walkways were the collapsed pair; this source-art gate remains pre-collapse only.

Visual implications:

- A reviewable generated backplate needs a clear stacked second/fourth pair, not generic multi-level balconies.
- A reviewable generated backplate also needs an offset third-floor suspended walkway.
- Hanger rods must attach to the walkway spans and read as structural, not as decorative strings.
- Tea dance event texture is required, but it cannot override the real suspended-walkway geometry.
