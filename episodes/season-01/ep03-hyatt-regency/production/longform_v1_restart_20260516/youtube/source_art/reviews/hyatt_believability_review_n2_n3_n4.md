## Judgment

N2 is the most believable overall image. It has the best photographic restraint, the least obvious generated repetition, and the strongest feeling of an actual event observed from above. Its problem is direction fit: the table balloon bouquets are smaller than the latest requested styling.

N3 is weaker. It begins to satisfy the taller bouquet direction, but the mixed small/large bouquet scale makes the table decor feel inconsistent and generated.

N4 is not production-safe as a believable source-art plate. It follows the uniform-large-bouquet instruction better than N3, but the repeated tall balloon groups, long string clusters, and uniform spacing make the scene feel more staged/generated than photographed. It solves the prompt while making the evidence feel less believable.

## Recommendation

Do not keep N4. Treat it as a tighten/reference-only pass.

Use N2 as the believability baseline for the next pass, but do not simply enlarge every balloon cluster. The next prompt should prioritize photographic plausibility over blanket decoration coverage:

- preserve N2/N architecture and crowd realism
- table decor should be believable tall centerpieces, but not mechanically identical
- remove tiny tabletop clusters only where they call attention to themselves
- avoid repeated balloon geometry, giant foreground balloon masses, and perfectly uniform string forests
- keep balloons as event texture, not evidence

The correct target is a believable hotel tea dance first, with balloons as plausible table decor second.
