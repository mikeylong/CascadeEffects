[matter-of-fact] A simple design change turned an approved structure into a death trap, and no one caught it because everyone assumed someone else had checked.

[matter-of-fact] Hyatt's original walkway design used one continuous rod to support two suspended walkways. During construction, that became two shorter rods, doubling the load on the critical connection.

[deliberate] The engineer approved it. The fabricator requested it. The inspector signed off.

[sorrowful] On July 17th, 1981, both walkways collapsed. 114 people died.

[calm] The connection had been carrying twice its designed load from day one. It wasn't one person's mistake. A structural change passed through multiple hands, and no one owned the final check.

[resigned] The Hyatt didn't collapse because of bad engineering. It collapsed because accountability had no address.
