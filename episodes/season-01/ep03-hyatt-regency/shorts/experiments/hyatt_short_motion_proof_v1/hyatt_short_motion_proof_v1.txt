[matter-of-fact] A simple design change turned a safe structure into a death trap, and no one caught it because everyone assumed someone else had checked.

[matter-of-fact] The original Hyatt Regency walkway design used a single continuous rod to support two suspended walkways. During construction, that was changed to two shorter rods, a modification that doubled the load on the critical connection point.

[deliberate] The engineer approved the change. The fabricator requested it. The inspector signed off.

[sorrowful] On July 17th, 1981, during a crowded lobby event, both walkways collapsed. 114 people died.

[calm] The connection had been carrying twice its designed load from day one. It wasn't one person's mistake. It was a system where a structural change passed through multiple hands and no one owned the final check.

[resigned] The Hyatt didn't collapse because of bad engineering. It collapsed because accountability had no address.
