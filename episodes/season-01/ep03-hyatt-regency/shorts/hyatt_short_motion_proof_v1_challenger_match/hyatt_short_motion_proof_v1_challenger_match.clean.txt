A simple design change turned an approved structure into a death trap, and no one caught it because everyone assumed someone else had checked.

Hyatt's original walkway design used one continuous rod to support two suspended walkways. During construction, that became two shorter rods, doubling the load on the critical connection.

The engineer approved it. The fabricator requested it. The inspector signed off.

On July 17th, 1981, both walkways collapsed. 114 people died.

The connection had been carrying twice its designed load from day one. It wasn't one person's mistake. A structural change passed through multiple hands, and no one owned the final check.

The Hyatt didn't collapse because of bad engineering. It collapsed because accountability had no address.
