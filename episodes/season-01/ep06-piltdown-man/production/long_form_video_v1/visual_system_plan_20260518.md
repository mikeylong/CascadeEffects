## Recognizability Ladder

- Public anchor: natural-history museum specimen table or display case with fossil fragments.
- Evidence object: separated human skull fragment, ape-like jaw, filed teeth, stained bone, and gravel-layer context.
- System clue: institutional plinths, casts, cabinet drawers, X-ray or fluorine-test light cues, and an anonymous museum worker silhouette.

No readable generated labels, museum logos, title text, watermarks, or real-person likenesses are allowed.

- Readable generated museum labels, logos, or text.
- Recognizable Charles Dawson, Arthur Smith Woodward, or any real person.

- Fossil fragments merged into one convincing creature instead of visibly separable evidence.

- Cyan paths that read as water, liquid, river, stream, or waterfall.
