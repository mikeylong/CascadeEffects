A patient received a radiation dose about one hundred times too high. The manufacturer said that was impossible. Then it happened again.

The Therac-25 replaced physical safety interlocks with software checks. During fast data entry, a race condition could leave the machine in the wrong state. The machine could fire a full-power beam without its filter. Nobody caught it because the code came from older machines. Reused code felt like tested code.

Operators reported errors. The manufacturer insisted the machine was safe. More patients were burned before the pattern was treated as real.

The disaster was not just bad code. It was a system with no independent safety layer. It had no quick way to believe field reports. And it had no design for when software was wrong.

That is how small causes become massive consequences.
