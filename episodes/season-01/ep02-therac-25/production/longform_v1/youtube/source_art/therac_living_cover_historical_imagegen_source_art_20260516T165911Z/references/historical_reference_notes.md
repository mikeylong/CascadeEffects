# Therac-25 Historical Reference Notes

## What changed from the superseded Paper Architecture pass

The new source-art lane is `cascade-ink-lit-photoreal-v1`, not Paper Architecture. The historical correction is to show the operational separation: patient alone in the shielded treatment room, operator outside at a period console/CRT, and a blocky Therac-era linear accelerator rather than a contemporary glossy linac or CT/imaging suite.

## Reference anchors

- Wikimedia Commons Therac-25 files: visual anchor for the broad Therac-era machine vocabulary and rotation/head relationship.
- Computing Cases machine-design and operator narrative: anchor for Therac lineage, treatment-mode/hardware concerns, and operator terminal workflow.
- Online Ethics Therac-25 narrative: cross-check for patient/operator separation, console/error-report context, and incident framing.
- Getty archival linear-accelerator motion and IAEA linac video: motion/workflow references for operator monitoring and radiotherapy-room grammar only; they are not treated as exact Therac-25 design evidence.

## Prompt constraints applied

- Anonymous patient, calm and non-suffering.
- Anonymous operator outside the treatment room at a CRT/console.
- No readable screens, labels, logos, text, paper details, or watermark-like marks.
- No gore, burns, distress, beam spectacle, Paper Architecture, or toy/model look.
- Strong right-side negative space for the fixed Living Cover rail.
