## Pulls By Episode Beat

### Cold Open: Enough By Law

- Use S1 for the compliance frame: Titanic's available boats were in compliance with UK laws then in force.
- Use S1 for the tonnage-table frame: requirements were based on ship tonnage, with the highest rule applying above 10,000 tons.
- Safe narration claim: "Titanic had enough lifeboats to satisfy the law."

### Measurement Failure

- Use S1 for the 1894/1902 rule lineage and the tonnage basis.
- Use S4 for parliamentary language that the regulations were out of date and needed revision for modern ships.
- Safe narration claim: "The law was measuring by category, not by the number of people who might need evacuation."

### Category Ceiling

- Use S1 for the over-10,000-ton, 16-lifeboat, 990-person requirement.
- Use S4 for the argument that the top category no longer matched the number and size of large ships by 1912.
- Safe narration claim: "Once every very large ship falls into the same highest bracket, the bracket stops doing useful measurement."

### Compliant Versus Sufficient

- Use S1 for Titanic carrying 16 lifeboats and 4 collapsibles, with total capacity stated as 1,176 in the U.S. Senate report and 1,178 in the British report.
- Use S1 for more than 2,200 aboard and more than 1,500 dead.
- Safe narration claim: "The ship carried more boat capacity than the rule required, but far less than the people aboard."

### Davits And Underreach

- Use S1 for the U.S. Senate report summary that Titanic's 16 sets of davits could handle up to 48 boats.
- Use S4 for parliamentary discussion that Olympic/Titanic could have carried 32 or 48 under the same davits.
- Safe narration claim: "The ship's hardware could imagine a larger emergency than the rule demanded."

### Evacuation As Adjacent Failure

- Use S1 for the British Commissioner finding that Titanic's boat accommodation was used to less than two-thirds of capacity.
- Use S6 for modern SOLAS lessons on lifeboat drill, training, loading, and public-address systems.
- Safe narration claim: "The law failed at capacity, and the evacuation failed differently in use."

### Institutional Aftermath

- Use S1 for the British recommendation to base lifeboat and raft accommodation on persons carried rather than tonnage.
- Use S5 for the official statement that the first SOLAS version was adopted in 1914 in response to Titanic.
- Use S6 for the broader official Titanic-to-SOLAS safety frame.
- Safe narration claim: "After Titanic, the institutional response moved toward the question the old table had not asked: what happens to the people aboard?"
