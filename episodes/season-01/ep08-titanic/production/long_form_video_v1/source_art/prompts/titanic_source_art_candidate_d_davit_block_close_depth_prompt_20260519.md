## Prompt

Generate a 16:9 horizontal source-art backplate for a Cascade Effects long-form Living Cover episode about Titanic's lifeboat regulation gap. Scene: close but still cinematic early-1910s Olympic-class ocean-liner boat deck, with a lifeboat davit block, pulley sheaves, falls, rope tension, boat edge, rail, and repeated davits receding into layered depth. Anonymous crew are present only as backs, cropped shoulders, gloved hands, or soft profiles engaged around the gear; no identifiable faces. The implied action is readiness and anticipation, not panic: lines are taut, a crewman is about to handle the fall, lamps are on, wet deck catches reflections, cold sea air and faint dawn light sit beyond the rail. Put machinery readiness and period maritime material in the left/center foreground and midground, while preserving quieter open sea/sky/rail space on the right for a future fixed right rail. Premium photoreal documentary style, source-preserving, historically anchored, no spectacle. No sinking, iceberg impact, panic, bodies, wreckage, smoke, debris, fire, breaking hull, or chaotic launching. No modern cruise ship elements, no modern lifeboats, no contemporary safety gear or signage. No readable ship name, no logos, no generated text, no UI, no arrows, no cyan traces, no overlays. No foreground clipboards, binders, folders, paperwork stacks, legal pads, trays, evidence tables, desks, or administrative props. No Paper Architecture, no folded paper, no foam-core model, no miniature diorama, no illustration-card style. Make it motion-ready for later: practical lamp micro-flicker, wet reflection shimmer, rope/fall tension accents, faint sea haze.
