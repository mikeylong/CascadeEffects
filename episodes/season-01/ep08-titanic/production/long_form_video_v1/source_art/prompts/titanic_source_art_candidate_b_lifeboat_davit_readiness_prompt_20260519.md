# Titanic Source-Art Candidate B Prompt

Use case: historical-scene
Asset type: Cascade Effects long-form Living Cover source-art backplate

Primary request:
Create a historically anchored, non-Paper-Architecture photoreal source-art backplate for the Titanic long-form episode. The image should show pre-disaster compliance and anticipation on an early-1910s Olympic-class ocean-liner boat deck: lifeboats, davits, falls, deck railings, funnels or vents in depth, and anonymous crew preparing or checking the lifeboat handling gear. The mechanism is the lifeboat regulation gap, shown through machinery readiness and empty deck capacity rather than paperwork.

Scene/backdrop:
An early-1910s transatlantic liner boat deck at cold dawn or blue-hour evening. The deck is damp from sea air, brass or painted deck hardware catches practical lamp glow, faint mist sits beyond the rail, and the sea/sky side of the frame stays quieter with broad layered negative space. No sinking, iceberg, panic, wreckage, smoke, debris, bodies, or disaster spectacle.

Subject:
One lifeboat and its davits are the visual anchor. The boat is secured but visually ready: ropes under light tension, blocks and falls visible, davit arms creating depth, a crew figure in period maritime clothing near the gear, and a few distant anonymous passengers or crew turned away or partially occluded. Faces must be unrecognizable. The image should feel like a system waiting to be tested, not a static evidence table.

Composition:
Wide fixed 16:9 cinematic frame. Layered depth from near davit hardware and lifeboat edge, through mid-deck crew activity, to distant deck rail, vents, funnel shapes, and sea haze. Keep the right side calmer and less cluttered through open deck, rail, sea, or sky forms so later Living Cover rail text can sit there. Do not draw or reserve a visible panel.

Motion affordance:
Build in credible ambient affordances for later Living Cover motion: practical deck lamps that could flicker subtly, damp deck reflections that could shimmer, faint cold mist beyond the rail, gently tensioned ropes, and quiet sea light. The image itself is still; the scene should imply imminent activity.

Style:
`cascade-ink-lit-photoreal-v1`, source-preserving documentary realism, restrained period color, plausible 1912 maritime materials, controlled contrast, calm but tense. No Paper Architecture, no folded-paper model, no foam-core props, no poster treatment, no UI, no graphic overlays, no cyan connector lines, no diagnostic marks.

Hard avoid list:
No foreground clipboards, binders, folders, paperwork stacks, legal pads, document trays, generic evidence-table props, admin desks, inspection paperwork as the visual anchor, readable text, ship names, logos, watermarks, labels, title lettering, modern cruise-ship features, modern lifeboats, modern safety gear, modern signage, face-forward people, recognizable faces, gore, disaster spectacle, iceberg strike, sinking, wreckage, smoke, debris, panic, or bodies.

Required review reads:
`foreground_admin_prop_read`, `implied_action_anticipation_read`, `ambient_affordance_read`, `historical_accuracy_read`, `source_reference_alignment_read`, `public_anchor_geometry_read`, `titanic_boat_deck_read`, `lifeboat_davit_accuracy_read`, `lifeboat_capacity_visual_logic_read`, `period_maritime_materials_read`, `disaster_spectacle_absence_read`, `human_presence_read`, `no_recognizable_faces_read`, `generated_text_logo_read`, `video_visual_style_scope_read`, `paper_architecture_visual_style_read`, `right_rail_safe_space_read`.
