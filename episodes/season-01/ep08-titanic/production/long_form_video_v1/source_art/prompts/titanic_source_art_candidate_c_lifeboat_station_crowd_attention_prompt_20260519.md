## Prompt

Generate a 16:9 horizontal source-art backplate for a Cascade Effects long-form Living Cover episode about Titanic's lifeboat regulation gap. Scene: early-1910s Olympic-class ocean-liner boat deck before disaster, several lifeboat stations and davits visible in layered depth, anonymous crew and passengers turned toward the lifeboats and cold horizon, damp deck reflections, practical deck lamps, rope falls under light tension, sea air and dawn/blue-hour light. The visual carrier should be historically anchored, photoreal/documentary, source-preserving, and motion-ready: lamps can flicker later, deck reflections can shimmer, ropes can subtly move, sea haze can drift, people can hold attention without identifiable faces. Compose with the strongest action and crowd attention in the left and center, with quieter open sea/sky/rail space on the right for a future fixed right rail. No disaster spectacle: no sinking, iceberg impact, panic, bodies, wreckage, smoke, debris, fire, breaking hull, or lifeboats launching in chaos. No modern cruise ship elements, no modern lifeboats, no modern signage, no contemporary safety gear. No readable ship name, no logos, no generated text, no UI, no arrows, no overlays. No foreground clipboards, binders, folders, paperwork stacks, legal pads, trays, evidence tables, desks, or administrative props. No Paper Architecture, no folded paper, no foam-core model, no miniature diorama, no illustration-card style. Avoid recognizable faces; people should be anonymous, back-turned or profile-soft. Premium photoreal maritime documentary tone, restrained palette, crisp geometry, layered depth, 1920x1080-ready.
