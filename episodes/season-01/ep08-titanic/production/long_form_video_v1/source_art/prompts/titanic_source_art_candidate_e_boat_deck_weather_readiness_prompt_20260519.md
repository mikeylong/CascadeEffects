## Prompt

Generate a 16:9 horizontal source-art backplate for a Cascade Effects long-form Living Cover episode about Titanic's lifeboat regulation gap. Scene: early-1910s Olympic-class ocean-liner boat deck in cold blue-hour weather, rows of lifeboats under canvas covers and period davits running into depth, deck lamps glowing through sea air, wet planked/steel deck reflecting light, anonymous crew silhouettes checking ropes and boat covers, passengers or stewards in the distance oriented toward the lifeboat row but not panicking. The frame should feel like quiet pre-disaster compliance and readiness before catastrophe, with visible empty deck capacity and repeated lifeboat stations making the regulation gap legible. Strong subject/event presence left and center; preserve open darker sea/sky/rail negative space on the right for a future fixed right rail. Premium photoreal documentary tone, historically anchored, source-preserving, restrained, no spectacle. Motion affordances for later: lamp micro-flicker, wet reflection shimmer, faint sea mist, rope/fall tension, subtle dawn/sea light. No sinking, iceberg impact, panic, bodies, wreckage, smoke, debris, fire, breaking hull, or chaotic launching. No modern cruise ship, no modern lifeboats, no modern signs, no contemporary safety gear. No readable ship name, no logos, no generated text, no UI, no arrows, no overlays. No foreground clipboards, binders, folders, paperwork stacks, legal pads, trays, evidence tables, desks, or administrative props. No Paper Architecture, no folded paper, no foam-core model, no miniature diorama, no illustration-card style. Avoid recognizable faces; people should be anonymous and back-turned or silhouette-soft.
