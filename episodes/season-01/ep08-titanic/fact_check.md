## Source Notes

- Library of Congress, "Failure to Update the Law: a Titanic Mistake" (2012): strongest compact source for compliance, tonnage rule, 16/990 requirement, 1,176/1,178 capacity split, davit capacity, under-use, Board of Trade criticism, and SOLAS bridge.
- Hansard, "BOARD OF TRADE - LOSS OF LIFE AT SEA" (21 May 1912): useful primary-era debate evidence for "out of date" regulation, large-liner scale change, davit/boat-capacity argument, and post-disaster assurances about enough boat or raft accommodation for all aboard.
- IMO SOLAS page: official source for first SOLAS adoption in 1914 in response to Titanic.
- IMO "Surviving disaster - The Titanic and SOLAS" PDF: official explainer for 1914 SOLAS response and modern SOLAS safety domains.
- U.S. Senate Titanic report PDF: authoritative source target, but the official PDF is image-based in local text extraction. Use the LOC article's Senate report excerpts unless OCR or a page-image review is performed later.
