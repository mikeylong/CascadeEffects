The 737 MAX was sold as a familiar airplane.

That was the commercial promise. Airlines could keep crews, schedules, and training close to the old 737.

Then Boeing put larger CFM LEAP engines on the aircraft. They sat farther forward and higher on the wing, and under certain high-angle-of-attack conditions, the jet could pitch up differently.

MCAS was built to make that new behavior feel familiar.

But the system could move the stabilizer from one angle-of-attack sensor. Pilots were not clearly trained that it existed by name.

After Lion Air 610, the warning still was not reclassified fast enough as an architectural problem.

The plane had changed. The story told to the people flying it had not.

Small causes, massive consequences.
