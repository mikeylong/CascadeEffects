## Episode Brief

- Primary lane: Design Failures That Changed the World.
- Mechanism: latent manufacturing/procedure defect.
- Public version: an oxygen tank exploded and the crew improvised their way home.
- Incomplete version: the crisis started long before the explosion, when tank history, ground-test procedure, heater voltage, switch design, and insulation damage created a hidden failure path.
- Audience promise: Apollo 13 was not just a heroic rescue; it was a systems survival problem triggered by a latent defect that launch operations did not surface.

## Causal Layers

- Hardware history: oxygen tank 2 carried prior handling and shelf-removal history.
- Ground test: detanking trouble led to heater use under conditions the thermostatic switches were not built to tolerate.
- Latent damage: overheated wiring insulation likely created a future ignition path inside the oxygen tank.
- Flight event: a routine tank fan stir likely triggered the short/spark/combustion chain.
- Survival system: oxygen loss disabled fuel-cell power and forced the lunar module into lifeboat service.

## Point Of No Return

The mission changed before the crew heard the bang: the damaged oxygen system had already crossed into a state where a routine fan stir could expose the latent fault.

## Compact Thesis

Apollo 13 became a survival story because a small ground-test mismatch hid inside a spacecraft system until flight turned it into a life-support cascade.

## 10-Part Spine

1. Open on the quiet preflight detail: tank 2 had a history before launch.
2. Establish the familiar public story only after the hidden mechanism is visible.
3. Show how ground testing and detanking converted a handling issue into latent damage.
4. Explain the oxygen tank, heater, switch, and Teflon insulation chain.
5. Move to the in-flight fan stir and the service-module damage.
6. Map the immediate cascade: oxygen, fuel cells, power, water, heat, navigation.
7. Turn to the lunar module as lifeboat, without making improvisation erase causation.
8. Show the evidence limits: no recovered service module, reconstruction by telemetry and testing.
9. Synthesize the system lesson: routine procedure can preserve a hidden state change.
10. Close on the altered mission: the crisis began before the explosion became visible.
