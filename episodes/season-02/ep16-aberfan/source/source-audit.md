## Writer-Packet Claims Supported

- On October 21, 1966, a coal spoil tip collapsed above Aberfan and engulfed Pantglas Junior School and nearby homes.
- 144 people died, including 116 children.
- The tribunal placed responsibility on the National Coal Board and treated the disaster as preventable.
- The reliable frame is visible environmental risk plus unclear ownership and institutional delay, not sudden natural accident.

## Cautions / Open Questions

- Avoid natural-disaster framing; rainfall/groundwater mattered, but the tip was an industrial waste structure.
- "Warnings ignored" needs precision: distinguish prior slips, drainage concerns, local complaints, and formal institutional knowledge.
- Do not let later royal/media memory displace the causal mechanism.
