Episode: Challenger Episode Acts

Failure mechanism:

- A long organizational drift from routine operations into normalized risk, culminating in a cold-weather O-ring seal failure and a failed institutional feedback loop.

Anchor fragment:

- The primary image anchor is the launch-commit control-room architecture, but it should appear as a cropped institutional shard rather than a literal room reconstruction.

Support fragments:

- Shuttle plume strips, field-joint geometry, winter ice, debris traces, circuit paths, and schematic overlays should layer around the anchor rather than collapse into a single hero subject.

Accent palette:

- Winter cream, smoked-glass blue, oxidized teal, charcoal, restrained flame orange, and pale lilac accents.

Safe-zone intent:

- Review board only. No packaging-safe band is required inside the sheet; preserve full tile reads and row grouping.

Disallowed motifs:

- No heroic astronaut portraiture or launch-poster triumph.
- No fireball spectacle as the primary emotional register.
- No paperwork close-ups whose meaning depends on readable lettering.
- No style references whose main value is a headline, cover title, or article page layout.
- No courtroom-drama shorthand or generic “scandal” imagery.
- No faux-retro sci-fi styling or glossy cyberpunk screens.

- Act 1, `Promise of routine` (`Ep1_Challenger.txt` lines 1-32): orderly mission-control and program-as-operations imagery.

- Act 3, `The Jan. 27 reversal` (`Ep1_Challenger.txt` lines 91-111): decision-space interiors, cold-weather cues, and burden-of-proof inversion.
- Act 4, `Launch and visible failure` (`Ep1_Challenger.txt` lines 113-123): launch frames, breach cues, and the first visible seconds of failure.
- Act 5, `Commission and pattern` (`Ep1_Challenger.txt` lines 125-167): investigation, recovered evidence, and the system-level coda.
