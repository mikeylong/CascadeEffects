# Casebook

This casebook turns recent proof packets into reusable judgment for future prompt writing.

Use these examples as evidence, not as prompt templates.

Current promotion state lives in `judgment/keeper_registry.md`.

## Disposition Key

- `keep`: usable proof result and current positive model for future work
- `tighten`: subject is viable, but camera, anomaly carrier, or motion concept still needs revision
- `diagnostic only`: useful for learning, not for final selection
- `reject`: failed output, usually because the anomaly reads as model damage or the subject stopped being canonical

## Cross-Case Takeaways

- `keep` results preserved the canonical subject and localized the anomaly.
- `tighten` results usually need camera, framing, or anomaly-carrier changes before they can graduate.
- `diagnostic only` results taught something useful but are not final candidates.
- `reject` results crossed from believable contradiction into AI slop.
- site and detail classes respond well when the anomaly carrier is environmental or mechanical rather than expressive or geometric.
- human-figure prompts can work, but screens and text-bearing interfaces remain unreliable anomaly carriers.
- passenger-aircraft prompts still need subsystem-specific camera strategies when the contradiction must be more legible than a side-profile can provide.
