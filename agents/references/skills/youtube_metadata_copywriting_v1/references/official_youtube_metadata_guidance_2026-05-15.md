## Practical Rules

YouTube search considers relevance, engagement, and quality. Relevance includes how well the title, tags, description, and video content match a search query. Engagement includes signals such as watch time for a query. The copy implication: metadata should match the video honestly, not bait the wrong click.

Titles and thumbnails are the primary viewer decision surface. YouTube recommends accurate titles, succinct wording, important words near the beginning, and branding or episode numbers near the end. Use all-caps and emoji sparingly.

Descriptions tell viewers and YouTube what the video is about. YouTube recommends using the first few lines for the actual video description because that is what viewers see first, making each video description unique, and featuring one or two main words naturally in both title and description. Descriptions can include chapters, playlists, links, collaborations, and channel information after the main description.

Platform limits from YouTube Help:

- Title: 100 characters.
- Description: 5,000 characters.
- Chapters entered in the description override automatic chapters.
- Tags are added in the upload/edit details flow under more options.

Tags are secondary. YouTube says title, thumbnail, and description are more important for discovery. Tags can help with common misspellings, but otherwise play a minimal discovery role. Do not put excessive tags into the description; YouTube identifies that as a spam/deceptive-practices risk.

## Cascade Effects Interpretation

- Write titles for the specific video promise first. Do not append `| Cascade Effects` to video titles because YouTube already displays the channel name throughout its templates and surfaces.
- Put the public explanation in the first description lines; put links, credits, and references after the chapters.
- Use tags for the incident, system, organization, mechanism, and likely search terms. Do not use tags as a bibliography.
- Keep scholar/source names out of public tags unless the video itself is about that person or the person is part of the title/description promise.
- Separate hashtags from tags. Hashtags should be rare and topical, not a substitute for metadata.
