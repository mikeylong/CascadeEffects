# Cascade Effects Illustration Contract

Active profile: `cascade-paper-architectures-ink-lit-v1`

This contract promotes ink-lit Paper Architectures as the canonical Cascade Effects illustration direction across web, YouTube, podcast, Shorts, long-form, and package assets. The former bright/daylight Paper Architectures look and `cascade-lowpoly-pastel-v1` are legacy/inactive reference, not defaults for new assets.

## Reference Evidence

The approved ink-lit Paper Architectures reference renders live at `brand/assets/reference-renders/paper-architectures-v1/` and are tracked through Git LFS. They are canonical visual evidence for deep ink-blue fields, cream paper forms, lavender paper shadows, cyan vellum glow, restrained coral accents, foam-core construction, controlled model lighting, and clean low-detail public-anchor staging.

Approved web/channel identity reference renders may include the `Cascade of Effects` title. Episode thumbnails, Shorts covers, chapter cards, and package source art remain text-free unless a package manifest explicitly permits title-bearing source art.

## Core Rule

Cascade Effects illustrations must read as luminous ink-lit Paper Architectures. Use tactile folded-paper systems, recognizable public anchors, and simple cyan cascade sheets or terraces to explain hidden system failures without becoming gritty documentary, noir, smoke, plastic toy, flat icon, or dense evidence-board art.

Website episode-gallery thumbnails are a narrower lane: the primary subject is the composition. For CascadeEffects.tv gallery cards, one dominant public subject overrides the broader three-level recognizability ladder because the card pill and title carry the episode context.

## Required Style

- Deep ink-blue fields, cream paper forms, lavender paper shadows, cyan vellum glow, restrained coral warning accents, and soft graphite seams.
- Folded paper planes, scored seams, subtle visible paper fibers, foam-core cut edges, translucent vellum, gouache/pastel pigment, and controlled model-light shadows.
- Clean low-detail geometry with readable public-anchor silhouettes.
- One or two paper terraces or cascade sheets maximum.
- For CascadeEffects.tv web episode-gallery thumbnails, one dominant public subject is required. Do not add separate evidence trays, ledgers, gauges, connection details, system diagrams, arrows, dry signal paths, or secondary prop clusters.

## Surface Texture Rule

- Surface texture must be minimal: subtle paper fiber only, clean low-detail paper planes, scored seams, foam-core edges, soft pigment, vellum translucency, and material shadows.
- Ban high-frequency speckle, visible grain, grit, sandy paper surfaces, noisy texture, texture-heavy rendering, AI shimmer, or any texture that competes with the public anchor or evidence object.
- Every generated source-art proof, package candidate, thumbnail, and motion-readiness candidate must record `texture_noise_read: pass|tighten|reject`.
- At `320x180` and `168x94`, texture must not become the dominant read or reduce public-anchor clarity. `texture_noise_read: tighten` or `texture_noise_read: reject` blocks `keep`, package promotion, thumbnails, rough assembly, and motion-readiness use.

## Required Subjects

- Public anchors: the recognizable case subject or setting.
- CascadeEffects.tv episode-gallery cards: use one dominant primary subject only; the website card pill/title carries the context.
- Evidence objects and system clues are for larger editorial, YouTube, podcast, long-form, and explicitly scoped package assets. They do not belong in web episode-gallery thumbnails unless explicitly requested for a new approved lane.
- Human figures are optional in small subject-only thumbnails and secondary everywhere else.

## Generation Rules

- Generate episode and package source artwork without readable text, logos, watermarks, or title lettering unless a manifest explicitly permits approved title-bearing identity art.
- For CascadeEffects.tv web episode-gallery thumbnails, start the prompt with the primary subject and explicitly ban secondary evidence/system props, arrows, diagrams, dry signal paths, and decorative cyan/coral artifacts.
- Compose `Cascade of Effects` locally after generation when a package needs title text, except where the manifest names an approved title-bearing identity reference.
- Keep public anchors large enough to read at thumbnail size.
- Keep the image luminous, tactile, clear, editorial, low-detail, and explicitly prompt: `subtle paper fiber only, clean low-detail paper planes, no speckle/noise/grain/grit/sandy texture`.
- Dark is allowed only as clean model-lit paper architecture: no noir, smoke, grit, evidence-board collage, or documentary mood.

## Deterministic Compositing Rule

Once a baseline illustration is approved, do not regenerate the full scene for small additions, removals, placement changes, visual gags, or texture-noise drift repair. Generate additions as isolated transparent or chroma-keyed layers, then composite them over the locked baseline with recorded source paths, placement, scale, rotation, opacity, and output path. If the baseline itself fails `texture_noise_read`, treat the replacement as a new baseline review.

Full-scene regeneration is allowed only when the art direction, format, crop, or core composition is intentionally being replaced and the new output is reviewed as a new baseline reference render.

## Prohibitions

- No photorealism, realistic water simulation, gritty texture, high-frequency speckle, visible grain, grit, sandy/noisy paper surfaces, texture-heavy rendering, dark documentary treatment, noir lighting, smoke, dense debris, logos, explosions, horror imagery, gore, disaster spectacle, plastic toy gloss, flat icon systems, CSS placeholder approximations, or mascot/kawaii styling.
- No decorative cyan or coral flags, tabs, ribbons, stickers, UI markers, tape scraps, colored tags, cubes, or warning accents in web episode-gallery thumbnails.
- No readable generated text unless an approved title-bearing identity-art manifest explicitly permits it.
- No bright/daylight Paper Architectures, low-poly plastic, archival evidence-board default, or old dark visual language unless a file is explicitly marked legacy/reference.

## QA

- At thumbnail scale, the public subject must remain recognizable at `320x180` and `168x94`.
- CascadeEffects.tv web episode-gallery thumbnails must remain subject-only: no separate evidence-object/system-clue clutter, no decorative cyan/coral artifacts, and no literal water language in live alt text.
- `texture_noise_read` must be `pass`; `tighten` or `reject` blocks package promotion, thumbnails, rough assembly, and motion-readiness use.
- Source renders must be text-free before deterministic composition unless the package manifest explicitly names an approved title-bearing source.
- The final asset must visibly fit the ink-lit Paper Architectures material system.
