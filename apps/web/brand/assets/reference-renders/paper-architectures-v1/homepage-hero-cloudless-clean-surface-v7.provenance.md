# Homepage Hero Cloudless Clean Surface V7 Provenance

Date: 2026-06-06

Scope: CascadeEffects.tv homepage hero smooth-paper raster/source-art revision with stronger peril and less ride-like figure staging.

Correction: v6 kept the smooth surface and darker title, but the seated upright figures and broad blue cascade panels could read as a water-park or amusement ride. v7 is a fresh text-prompt image-generation rebuild, not an image-to-image iteration from v6/v5. It preserves the title-bearing identity scene while rebuilding the cascade as buckling dry vellum/paper architecture with torn lips, collapsed folds, broken ledges, and alarmed partially occluded figures.

Supersession: v7 is no longer the active homepage hero because the launch tower/pad picked up too much of the purple title color. Use `homepage-hero-cloudless-clean-surface-v8.provenance.md` and its regenerated v8 source renders for the active web hero. v7 remains the peril/staging rollback reference.

## Asset Brief

- Archetype: Folded Cascade Model.
- Carrier: built-in image generation from a written prompt only, with exact-size local runtime derivations.
- Title/text policy: preserve only the baked `Cascade of Effects` folded-paper title; no extra copy, labels, logos, or watermarks.
- Composition: preserve the title, broken suspension bridge, folded paper mountain/cascade stage, three endangered paper figures, shuttle stack, launch tower, leaning ocean liner, and ink-blue field.
- Peril treatment: figures are alarmed, clinging, reaching, and partially swallowed or occluded by buckled dry vellum sheets rather than sitting upright or sliding feet-first.
- Smooth-paper treatment: clean folded paper/vellum sheets with broad planes, soft scored seams, foam-core cut edges, controlled model lighting, and subtle paper fiber only.
- Surface rule: stone, rock, ice, crystalline facets, rough plaster, concrete, visible grain, speckle, grit, sandy surface, noisy shadows, texture overlays, and texture competing with title or scene readability are blockers.
- Signal rule: blue cascade forms must read as dry cyan vellum/paper sheets, not water, waterfall, river, stream, liquid spill, flowing channel, reflective pool, or recreational chute.
